Recently, I suddenly had the opinion thag I should run Linux on my MacBook Air M1.

I looked around and discovered that the project with the best support was Asahi Linux. 
I was happy that some people had taken time make it possible to run Linux natively on a MacBook M1.

Unfortunately, upon further research, I came across a blog post from the lead maintainer, "Hector Martin" announcing in a blog post that he was [resigning from being the Asahi Linux Project Lead](https://marcan.st/2025/02/resigning-as-asahi-linux-project-lead/)

The _Too Long Don't Read_ version of the blog post is "donations dwindling, too much toxicity & I am experiencing burn out".

I have seen this pattern occurring in many projects. Everyone like free software, tools etc. But the reality is that they are all human, and humans have limits. 

Once strange experience I have had in these communities is that, whenever I suggest an economic incentive be built around these projects. I get hostile replies saying I am an evil vampire.

## Death of Mastodon Instances

I suspect the absence of a proper monetisation strategy is why Mastodon instances shit down so often.

It is as if Mastodon instances on the other hand, are hardwired to fail.

- If a community is small, traffic is low and the self hosted instance is cheap
- If a community grows to a very large size (becomes successful) self-hosting costs balloon to unsustainable levels and the instance needs to shutdown. 

Altruism has its limits, it is not sustainable to run a project off passion alone.
