In early 2025, the Vatican wrote an interesting, albeit, very long essay on artificial intelligence titled "ANTIQUA ET NOVA".  
  
It's latin for "Old and New", with the subtitle, "Note on the Relationship Between  
Artificial Intelligence and Human Intelligence".  
  
The essay references the ideas of St. Thomas Aquinas, a theological giant in Catholicism.  
  
A few people in the computer technology industry seem to take Christian theology seriously. The most popular may be author Donald Knuth and venture capitalist Peter Thiel.  
  
But today I want outline a few relationships between Theology and Artificial Intelligence.

George Boole, Modern Computing & AGI  
  
Boolean Algebra, which is eponymous to George Boole (it's inventor) may be regarded as the foundation of digital computers.  
  
However what is lesser known is that George Boole suggested that his ideas, in the book "The Laws of Thought" where actually a description on how the human brain works.  
  
The holy grail of AI, is the so called Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) which can be defined as fully mimicking human cognitive abilities on a computer so that computers can do what everything humans conceivably can do.  
  
George Boole's "The Laws of Thought" can then be interpreted as probably the first attempt to formalise how an AGI would work.  
  
Relating this to theology, it is said that, "He had a spiritual experience as a teenager, which he interpreted as a divine call to explain and elucidate the mathematical laws of thought."
