Atrophy is defined on Wikipedia as _the partial or complete wasting away of a part of the body_. [^1]

This often happens when an organ is not used as much. So maybe the body wastes it way internationally as it may not “want” to keep on maintaining something that it isn’t using.

This happens with muscles. For instance, when you stop working out at the gym, your muscles start to slowly waste away.

What worries me is that this may happen to skilled programmers brains. The developed faculties of the brain concerning temporal logic [^2] might be wasted away such that;

**a)** Other tasks that might have benefited from your “programming cognitive muscles” might get harder to do.

**b)** It might get harder to learn a new “programming like” skill that shows up in the future.

In London for example, they have a taxi service informally referred to as “Black Cabs”. It turns out that in order to work for these “Black Cabs” you are required to memorize over 40,000 streets, landmarks and major buildings. [^3]

People who qualify to drive for black cabs often develop a bigger hippocampus (the part of the brain responsible for memory), and this bigger hippocampus probably helps them memorize stuff more effectively compared to Uber drivers that rely on GPS [^4]

A better memory system will also be beneficial to the "black cab drivers" for tasks that have nothing to do with driving, but require good memory.

Everyone is talking about what LLMs will offer them. Unfortunately, there is little discussion on what they will take away. Atrophy risk of cognitive skills amongst vibe coders is something IMHO that should be looked at more closely.

[^2]: [Temporal Logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temporal_logic) is the system in which I suspect programmers use to think about code. It is essentially a way of thinking about how something changes or behaves though time.
